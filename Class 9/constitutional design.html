'Republic' denotes that the head of state is elected and not hereditary. Adopted on November 26, 1949, and effective from January 26, 1950, the Constitution is the supreme law of India and can be amended to adapt to changing conditions.

Q4: Discuss the key reasons why countries need a constitution, highlighting its role in protecting citizens' rights and shaping governance. IMPORTANT 5 Marks
Answer:
Countries need a constitution for several reasons:
1. **Establishing Fundamental Principles**: It lays down the basic rules and values that guide the functioning of the state and society.
2. **Protecting Citizens' Rights**: The constitution guarantees fundamental rights such as freedom of speech, equality, and protection against discrimination, ensuring that citizens are treated fairly.
3. **Limiting Government Power**: It defines the scope of governmental authority, preventing misuse of power and ensuring accountability.
4. **Conflict Resolution**: The constitution provides mechanisms to resolve disputes between different branches of government or between citizens and the state.
5. **Adapting to Change**: It allows for amendments to address evolving societal needs and challenges.
By fulfilling these roles, the constitution serves as the backbone of governance and democracy, ensuring stability and justice in the country.
Q5: Explain the role of the Constituent Assembly in the making of the Indian Constitution. How was it formed, and what were its key functions? IMPORTANT 4 Marks
Answer:
The Constituent Assembly played a pivotal role in the making of the Indian Constitution. It was formed in 1946 under the Cabinet Mission Plan. The Assembly consisted of representatives elected by the provincial legislatures and nominated by princely states. Its key functions included drafting the Constitution, debating its provisions, and ensuring that it reflected the aspirations of a diverse population. The Assembly was chaired by Dr. Rajendra Prasad, while Dr. B.R. Ambedkar served as the Chairman of the Drafting Committee. The Assembly worked tirelessly for nearly three years, from December 1946 to November 1949, to create a comprehensive document that laid the foundation for democratic governance in India.

• Free and Fair Electoral Competition in Democracy
One of the essential features of democracy is free and fair electoral competition. This ensures that citizens have the right to choose their representatives through regular elections conducted in a transparent manner. Elections must provide equal opportunities for all political parties and candidates to contest. The process should be free from coercion, fraud, or manipulation. For example, universal adult franchise, which grants voting rights to all citizens aged 18 and above, is a key component of democratic elections. The concept of free and fair elections is critical for distinguishing democratic governments from non-democratic ones.
└─ 📝 One person, one vote, one value
• One Person, One Vote, One Value
The principle of 'One Person, One Vote, One Value' is a fundamental feature of democracy. It ensures political equality by granting every individual the right to vote, with each vote carrying equal weight. This concept is a cornerstone of democratic governance, distinguishing it from non-democratic systems where voting rights may be restricted or unequal. For example, in South Africa during the apartheid era (1948–1994), the principle was violated as only white citizens had full voting rights, while the majority black population was denied this equality. The adoption of universal adult franchise, as seen in India in 1950, is a key example of implementing this democratic principle.

• Rule of Law and Respect for Rights in Democracy
The rule of law is a fundamental principle of democracy, ensuring that all individuals, including those in positions of power, are subject to the same laws. This concept guarantees equality before the law and protects individual rights. Democracy emphasizes respect for rights such as freedom of speech, equality, and the right to vote. Answer:
Democracy is a form of government in which the rulers are elected by the people. Its key features include:
1. **Elected Representatives**: Leaders are chosen through free and fair elections where citizens vote. For example, India conducts regular elections to elect its representatives.
2. **Universal Adult Franchise**: Every adult citizen has the right to vote, irrespective of caste, gender, or religion.

Q3: Explain the key features of democracy and how they distinguish it from non-democratic forms of government. IMPORTANT 5 Marks
Answer:
Democracy is characterized by several key features that set it apart from non-democratic forms of government. First, democracy ensures the participation of citizens in decision-making processes, either directly or through elected representatives. This principle of popular sovereignty is central to democracy. Second, it guarantees fundamental rights and freedoms, such as freedom of speech, equality, and the right to vote, which are essential for the functioning of a democratic society. Third, democracy operates on the rule of law, ensuring that all individuals, including those in positions of power, are subject to the law. Fourth, it promotes accountability and transparency, as elected leaders are answerable to the people and can be replaced through regular, free, and fair elections. In contrast, non-democratic governments often lack these features, with power concentrated in the hands of a few, limited or no citizen participation, and restricted rights and freedoms.

Q6: Discuss the limitations of democracy as a form of government. Why do some critics argue that democracy is not always the best way to govern? 5 Marks
Answer:
While democracy has many advantages, it also has limitations that critics often highlight:
1. Decision-making delays: Democratic processes, such as debates and elections, can slow down decision-making.
2. Risk of majority rule: Sometimes, the majority may overlook the rights and needs of minority groups.
3. Influence of money and power: Wealthy individuals or groups can manipulate elections and policies, undermining equality.
4. Lack of informed participation: Citizens may not always be well-informed about policies or candidates, leading to poor decision-making.
Critics argue that in certain situations, such as emergencies, authoritarian systems may provide quicker and more decisive governance. However, these limitations do not outweigh the benefits of democracy, which remains the most inclusive and fair system overall.

5. **Economic Inequality**: Despite political equality, democracies often fail to address economic disparities. Wealthy individuals or corporations may influence policies through lobbying or campaign funding.

Critics argue that in certain situations, other forms of government, such as technocracies or benevolent dictatorships, may be more effective in achieving rapid development or maintaining stability. However, proponents of democracy counter that its ability to adapt and self-correct outweighs these limitations.
